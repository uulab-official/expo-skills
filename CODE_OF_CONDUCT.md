# Code of Conduct

This project exists to help people build Expo apps with practical, reusable agent skills. We want the repository to stay useful, welcoming, and safe for contributors with different backgrounds and experience levels.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but accept correction when impact differs from intent.
- Keep discussions focused on the work: skills, docs, examples, safety, and verification.
- Give actionable feedback with enough context for another contributor to improve the work.
- Protect private information. Do not post tokens, passwords, private keys, service account JSON, keystores, provisioning profiles, unreleased app identifiers, or personal contact details.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Pressuring contributors to reveal credentials, account access, or private project details.
- Repeatedly derailing issues or pull requests after maintainers ask to refocus.
- Submitting examples that contain real secrets or private customer data.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments and contributions that violate this code. Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation when needed to protect the project and its contributors.

If you need to report a conduct issue, contact the maintainers through the repository's normal issue or discussion channels. If the issue includes private security-sensitive information, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead and do not post sensitive details publicly.
